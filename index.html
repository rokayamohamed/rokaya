Experiences

  • Build in a Box – BUILDing an Entrepreneurial Ecosystem Across Africa (BIAB) by the African Leadership Academy

    Africa's young population, with almost half under 25, is both an asset and a challenge. This demographic brings growth opportunities but also social issues. Youth can invigorate markets and innovation, yet they can also strain infrastructure and contribute to crime. The formal job sector can't accommodate many young people, especially those aged 15-22, leading to high youth unemployment in key African economies. To address this, entrepreneurship is seen as a solution, but starting a business at a young age is tough, and youth-focused support is lacking. In response, the African Leadership Academy (ALA) has introduced the BUILD-in-a-Box Camp, offering critical entrepreneurial training to youth aged 15-22. The program employs the BUILD curriculum, teaching youth to Believe in their ability to make change, Understand their communities, Invent solutions, Listen to feedback, and ultimately Deliver impactful ventures. The BUILD-in-a-Box toolkit allows ALA to extend its reach beyond its doors. With support from The MasterCard Foundation and The Blackstone Charitable Foundation, ALA plans to host 27 BUILD-in-a-Box camps led by young leaders in various African cities, targeting unemployed youth and fostering an entrepreneurial mindset. This initiative aims to create opportunities for youth and establish a network of trained facilitators to support young entrepreneurs across cities. It represents a youth-led solution to a critical challenge, believing in Africa's youth to unlock their potential. I spent over 16 hours at the two-day boot camp, collaborating with my team, Group C, to tackle the SDG on quality education. I was honored to receive the Best Speaker Award among +50 participants.

    Date: Jul - Aug '23
    Location: Cairo, Egypt
